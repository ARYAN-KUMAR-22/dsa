Divide & Conquer Approach

Instead of checking every subarray (Brute Force O(n³)), divide the problem recursively and combine smaller solutions. This approach teaches fundamental D&C patterns!

Three Cases

1. Max in LEFT half: Recursively solve left side
2. Max in RIGHT half: Recursively solve right side
3. Max CROSSES midpoint: Find best from both sides that meet at mid

Crossing Subarray (Key!)

The tricky part: subarray that spans LEFT and RIGHT.
• Find best ending at mid-left (sum from mid leftward)
• Find best starting at mid+1 (sum from mid+1 rightward)
• Combine them!

Complexity

T(n) = 2T(n/2) + O(n)
By master theorem: O(n log n)
Not as good as Kadane's O(n), but shows powerful D&C pattern!

When to Use

• Learning Divide & Conquer pattern
• Similar problems requiring D&C (closest pair, etc)
• Not for production (Kadane's is simpler + O(n))
• Great for interviews to show algorithmic thinking!
