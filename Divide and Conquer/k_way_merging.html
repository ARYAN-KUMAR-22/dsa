Common Mistakes

          

            
Forgetting sorted inputs
K-way merging assumes each input list is already sorted; otherwise the chosen minimum head is not globally safe.

            
Not advancing the right pointer
After outputting one value, only the chosen list's pointer should move forward.

            
Choosing the wrong strategy
A naive scan is easy to write, but for large k it wastes work compared with a heap or pairwise merge tree.

            
Interview framing
State that you always pick the smallest current head, then explain how you make that choice efficient.
