Decision Playbook

            

              
Use divide and conquer when
The subproblems are mostly independent, the base case is tiny, and the combine step is controlled.

              
Switch to dynamic programming when
Subproblems overlap and memoization can reuse work that recursion would repeat.

              
Switch to greedy when
A provably safe local choice can replace the whole recursive split.
